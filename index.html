The following application displays the data and results associated with the Misperception of Extreme Weather Event Mortality Risk in the United States manuscript, a forthcoming research letter from the CHENLab and Yale Program on Climate Change Communication (YPCCC).

The analysis aims to describe and quantify the gap between percieved and actual risk of mortality due to extreme weather in United States' counties. The extreme weather events inlcude wildfire smoke, heat, flood, and drought, stemming from multiple previous CHENLab publications (see references). Cumulative mortality rate is calculated by summing the individual event-specific mortality rates. Public percpetion data, including the percentage of adults who think that global warming "will harm me personally" and "is happening", come from the YPCCC's Yale Climate Opinion Maps 2024.
