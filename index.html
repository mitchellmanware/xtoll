Instructions

Click on a county to view it's national rankings for wildfire smoke-, heat-, cold-, drought-, and flood-related and total extreme weather event-related mortality in the "County Profile". A selection will update the "Excess Mortality Rate Ranking (National)" bar chart, and "Dollars Lost to Extreme Weather-Related Mortality" plot to state-specific values based on the state in which the county is located. State values are used for calculating dollars lost to protect potentially identifiable health outcomes at the county level.

The mortality rate rankings data can be downloaded by selecting the "Download (GeoJSON)" link at the bottom of the sidebar.

References

See the following publications for more information on the methods used to estimate the county-level extreme weather event mortality rates used for these rankings.

Wildfire Smoke PM2.5: Ma et al., 2024
Heat and Cold: Chu et al., 2025(a)
Drought: Hu et al., 2025
Flood: Chu et al., 2025(b)
